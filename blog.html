Responsive Design

The design of mobile web layouts for smartphones poses a challenge for designers time and time again. The little space available on the devices has to be used in a meaningful way – mostly for the content. Thus, it would be best if the navigation took up no space at all, while still being easy and intuitive to find. The following examples show you suggestions where to place the navigation in your mobile web designs.Those that don’t want to hide the entire navigation outside of the viewport will surely enjoy the following navigation. It’s a classic horizontal navigation with the menu items sorted by priority – as it should be anyway. The first menu item is the most important one. The other items follow in descending significance.Since, especially with extensive websites, it is unlikely that all menu items will fit into navigation, you simply hide everything that doesn’t fit in, and add a “more” button at the end of the navigation. Behind that, there’s a drop-down menu containing all the remaining links of your navigation. The advantage is that not the entire navigation is hidden. Everything that fits is displayed. Usually, all the most important menu items are displayed. This flexible navigation is a mix of completely visible, and completely hidden.
